HALAL CERTIFICATE

Every product must have a halal certificate.

There are two options for obtaining a halal certificate:

  • Certification by a foreign halal certification body that has established a Mutual Recognition Agreement (MRA) with BPJPH in Indonesia.
  • Direct certification by BPJPH, which involves an on-site audit of the production facility.

If a halal certificate is obtained through a foreign certification body, the validity period follows the regulations of the respective halal body. If the halal certification is issued directly by BPJPH, the certificate remains valid for a lifetime, provided there are no changes to the ingredients.

BPOM REGISTRATION

Required Documents:

  • Letter of Agreement (signed & stamped by Chamber of Commerce)
  • Health Certificate
  • Artwork
  • Product Specification & Packaging Specification
  • Percentage of Ingredients with a total of 100%
  • Food Safety Certificate (GMP/HACCP/ISO 22000/IFS/BRC)
  • Technical Sheet of Ingredients or MSDS
  • Production Process (Flow Chart)
  • Production Code (meaning) dd/mm/yyyy
  • Product Photo (All Size Available)
  • Product Laboratory Testing

Ensure all conditions are fulfilled based on the applicable standards.
